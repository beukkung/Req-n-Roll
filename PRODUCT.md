# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Business analysts, product managers, product owners, and requirement practitioners in an internal beta. They use Req'n Roll to practice BA thinking, requirement analysis, stakeholder communication, and certification-style exam readiness in Thai-first workflows.

## Product Purpose

Req'n Roll is a lightweight practice tool for BA and requirements work. It combines daily requirement questions, skill assessment, reusable templates, Req Gym practice sets, XP, badges, and leaderboard-style motivation so users can build practical confidence through repeated drills.

Success means users can quickly start a practice session, understand why an answer is correct, download usable requirement templates, track their progress by nickname, and keep practicing without needing email sign-in or a full backend session.

## Brand Personality

Practical, energetic, supportive. The app should feel like a focused training gym for requirement skills: warm enough to reduce fear, structured enough to feel credible, and game-like only where it reinforces practice.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS marketing pages, decorative card grids that do not support tasks, exam-prep pages that feel fake or thin, and playful visuals that reduce trust in the BA/certification content. Avoid UI that looks like a demo shell instead of a usable practice tool.

## Design Principles

1. Practice starts fast: every page should lead to a clear next action.
2. Feedback teaches: explanations, scores, and progress should help users improve, not just celebrate.
3. Gamification supports credibility: XP, badges, and mascots should motivate without overpowering the learning task.
4. Thai-first clarity: Thai copy should be readable, specific, and task-oriented.
5. Offline-safe beta: Supabase-backed features should degrade gracefully to static content and local progress when env vars are missing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for the public UI. Keep Thai text legible, preserve keyboard access across quizzes and dialogs, provide visible focus states, respect reduced motion preferences, and avoid color-only feedback for quiz correctness, badges, and progress states.
